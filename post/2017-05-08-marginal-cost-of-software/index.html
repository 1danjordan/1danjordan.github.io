The Marginal Cost of Software

 May 1, 2017 economics quote oss

Recently I was listening to the podcast Opensource Craft, on which Dirk Eddelbuettel was a guest. Dirk is a prolific contributor to R community. Just check out his Github and his insane commit history. Dirk’s PhD is in econometrics, and on this podcast he makes a very insightful point, drawing from his experience of economics and OSS development, that I wanted to preserve here:

In economics a thing we get into the heads of first year students is basically, that everything changes until you hit an equilibrium of ‘marginal cost equals the marginal benefit’. Software is a really different beast, because open source software has no marginal cost. Making an additional copy has no cost.

Dirk Eddelbuettel

The fact that open source software has no marginal cost is profound.
